## Concept

OOH serves a single strategic function in this campaign: **make "Safe Punk" legible in physical space before the target audience encounters it digitally.** When a Cultural Native sees the campaign on the MTR at 8am, then encounters Laszlo Bot on Telegram at noon, then sees the Pizza Day countdown on Twitter at 6pm — the brand has become unavoidable without being intrusive.

The creative logic reverses the conventional OOH formula. Most financial brand OOH is clean, credibility-signalling, and conservative. HashKey's OOH is its inverse: neon-on-concrete visual language inside the city's institutional arteries. The medium (premium MTR lightbox, Causeway Bay LED wall) is the compliance signal; the creative (degen vocabulary, Laszlo voice, countercultural copy) is the culture signal. The contradiction is the message.

**Campaign window:** Phase 1, 1 February — 21 May 2026  
**Phase 2 special placement:** Hoardings around the Central Pizza Day event site, 15–22 May

## Three Creative Directions

### Direction A — "Testimony" (MTR Central / Long-form copy)

**Format:** MTR concourse lightbox, 2-panel sequential  
**Visual:** Dark near-black background. Single column of white text, left-aligned. Neon pink accent line at top.

**Panel 1 copy:**
```
In 2010 I spent
10,000 Bitcoin
on two pizzas.

Everyone said I was wrong.

— Laszlo
```

**Panel 2 copy:**
```
In 2026 you put
your assets on a
licensed exchange.

Everyone said you were boring.

Compliance Can Be Punk.
HASHKEY EXCHANGE    hashkey.com/pizza
```

**Strategic logic:** The MTR Central commuter is exactly Segment B (Cautious Explorer) — financially literate, 25–35, sees institutional messaging daily. Running a first-person historical voice in that context is a pattern interrupt. "Everyone said you were boring" is the validation this segment needs.

---

### Direction B — "Numbers" (Causeway Bay DOOH / Minimal)

**Format:** Causeway Bay digital billboard, 15-second loop  
**Visual:** 3-second black screen → number appears in neon cyan → tagline fades in → QR

**Sequence:**
```
[0–3s]   Black. Silence.

[3–8s]   10,001
         (neon cyan, full screen, no other text)

[8–12s]  The number after the legend.
         22.05.2026
         (white, smaller)

[12–15s] HASHKEY EXCHANGE
         Compliance Can Be Punk.
         [QR → hashkey.com/pizza]
```

**Strategic logic:** Causeway Bay's 1M daily traffic (Adintime, 2026) skews younger and more culturally engaged than Central. A full-screen number with no explanation rewards cultural literacy — 63.1% of the target audience knows what it refers to (Section 2.2.1). Those who don't are curious enough to scan.

---

### Direction C — "Permission" (Sham Shui Po flyposting / Street)

**Format:** A3 riso-print-aesthetic posters, 0xU sticker overlays, QR flyposting  
**Visual:** Photocopied aesthetic, black on yellow or pink on black. Deliberately lo-fi to signal community origin, not brand broadcast.

**Copy options (mix across locations):**
```
你嘅錢喺邊個手上？
(Your money. Whose hands?)
0xU  —  Institutional Degen Union
hashkey.com/pizza
```

```
FTX 冇SFC牌照。
HashKey 有。
去搞清楚先。
(FTX had no SFC licence.
HashKey does.
Sort that out first.)
```

```
COMPLIANCE
CAN BE
PUNK.
22.05.2026
```

**Strategic logic:** Sham Shui Po is explicitly named in Section 4.2.5 as an 0xU flyposting location alongside Central. The lo-fi aesthetic is non-negotiable — if this looks like a paid brand campaign, it fails. It must look like the community put it up. This direction is designed to generate LIHKG posts ("見到呢張poster喺深水埗") that brands cannot buy.
