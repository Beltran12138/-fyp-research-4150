## Concept

Pizza Personas are not mascots. They are mirrors.

The original persona design was structured around the campaign's compliance theme — each character embodied a different "relationship with regulation." In development, this approach was identified as a strategic error: a compliance-centric identity framework is legible to the brand team but invisible to the target community. A persona system that requires the audience to care about licensing before they care about the character will not generate organic sharing.

The revised framework draws from the SBTI (Silly Behavioral Type Indicator) model, which became the dominant viral personality mechanic on Chinese social platforms in April 2026, generating 40.85 million WeChat searches in a single day (TechNode, 2026). SBTI's core insight, as documented by TechNode's analysis, is that its results function "less like a tool for self-understanding and more like an instantly legible social joke" — users recognise the label, laugh, and repost not because it is accurate but because it is *true*. The mechanic operates as "a snapshot of how they feel they are functioning at a given moment," not a psychological assessment.

Applied to the crypto community, this means five personas grounded in **observable community behaviours on Crypto Twitter/X** — the specific, recognisable, slightly embarrassing things every crypto participant does. The campaign's compliance positioning enters through the result cards' framing, not through the persona design itself. Recognition precedes message.

**Three strategic functions:**

1. **Community recognition engine** — personas based on actual crypto Twitter archetypes generate the "係我！" (that's me) moment that drives sharing without requiring any brand awareness
2. **UGC flywheel** — result cards are designed as native screenshots; sharing them is the social action, not a CTA to an external link
3. **Meme seed** — "你係Hawaiian未？" (Are you Hawaiian yet?) becomes the campaign's organic question, replicable by any community member without brand involvement

---

## 5.7.1 Design Framework: Community Mirror, Not Brand Mirror

SBTI's viral mechanics are structurally distinct from MBTI-style personality tests. Where MBTI asks "what kind of person are you fundamentally?", SBTI asks "what are you doing right now that is slightly ridiculous?" The former requires psychological investment; the latter requires only recognition.

The Pizza Personas quiz operates on the same principle:

| Dimension | Traditional Persona Approach | SBTI-informed Approach (this campaign) |
|---|---|---|
| Persona basis | Brand values / compliance attitudes | Observable community behaviours on Crypto Twitter/X |
| Identification mechanism | "I believe in X" | "I have literally done this" |
| Sharing motivation | Self-expression | Social joke / collective recognition |
| Compliance message | Foregrounded | Embedded in result framing |
| Community origin signal | Branded | Feels community-generated |

The five personas are named after pizza varieties — maintaining the campaign's foundational Bitcoin Pizza Day narrative — but each character's identity is built from crypto community behaviour patterns, not brand messaging. The pizza name is the label; the crypto behaviour is the recognition.

## 5.7.2 The Five Personas

---

### Persona 1 — 等跌黨 / THE DIP WAITER 🍕
*Pizza type: Neapolitan (still waiting for the perfect temperature)*

> **"BTC跌番兩萬我先入。唔急。"**
> — You. When BTC was $78,000.

**Behaviour profile:** Waits for the dip every cycle. The dip arrives; they wait for a deeper dip. Misses the entire bull run. FOMO in at ATH. Attributes the subsequent crash to bad timing rather than the strategy itself. Repeats next cycle.

**Crypto Twitter signature:** Posts "still waiting" threads with increasingly specific target prices. Disappears during bull markets. Reappears during bear markets to explain why they were right to wait.

**Why people share this result:** Recognition is instant and universal. Every crypto community has multiple Dip Waiters. Receiving this result is a public self-roast that generates replies from friends who recognise the pattern.

**Result card punchline:** *你係唔係而家都仲喺度等？*
/ *Are you still waiting right now?*

---

### Persona 2 — 截圖選手 / P&L EXHIBITIONIST 📸
*Pizza type: Four Seasons (only the best sections are shown)*

> **推特：+340% 🚀🚀🚀**
> **實際：[已刪除]**

**Behaviour profile:** Posts winning trades with screenshots. Losing trades are silently deleted. Has never, in recorded history, posted a -80% screenshot. Gas fees are never mentioned. The "just testing" 100x leverage position does not exist in the public record.

**Crypto Twitter signature:** Appearance on your timeline correlates perfectly with bull market conditions. During bear markets: silence, or pivots to posting about fundamentals.

**Why people share this result:** The screenshot culture is universal in crypto communities. Receiving this result is an admission that costs something — which makes it shareable as social honesty.

**Result card punchline:** *你係唔係從來唔發虧損截圖？*
/ *Have you ever posted a losing trade?*

---

### Persona 3 — 事後先知 / RETROACTIVE PROPHET 🔮
*Pizza type: Diavolo (spicy in retrospect)*

> **「我年頭就話SOL會漲。」**
> （無記錄。但係真嘅。）

**Behaviour profile:** Perfect prediction record, all delivered after the event. Appears in every major price movement thread to explain that they knew. Has never posted a prediction before it happened. The absence of timestamps is not considered relevant evidence.

**Crypto Twitter signature:** Long analytical threads that begin with "as I said previously" without linking to the previous post. Specific about prices, vague about dates.

**Why people share this result:** Every crypto community has someone who fits this exactly. Sharing the result is a coded message to friends: "we all know who this is."

**Result card punchline:** *你係唔係永遠係事後先開口？*
/ *Do you always speak up after it happens?*

---

### Persona 4 — 撸毛黨 / AIRDROP FARMER ⛏️
*Pizza type: Supreme (every topping, every protocol)*

> **「我有47個錢包。**
> **上週Gas費: $340**
> **空投到手: $280**
> **利潤: 我唔計㗎。」**

**Behaviour profile:** Treats airdrop farming as full-time employment. Maintains 40+ wallets across multiple chains. Participates in every protocol interaction regardless of likelihood of reward. Has a spreadsheet. The spreadsheet does not include a net profit/loss column.

**Crypto Twitter signature:** Converts every timeline into an airdrop tutorial. Replies to project announcements with "is there an airdrop?" within minutes. Has "DeFi researcher" in bio.

**Why people share this result:** The Airdrop Farmer archetype is celebrated in crypto culture as industrious, if slightly delusional. Sharing this result signals community membership and self-awareness simultaneously.

**Result card punchline:** *你係唔係仲喺撸緊毛？*
/ *Are you still farming right now?*

---

### Persona 5 — 已覺醒者 / THE RECLAIMED 🍍
*Pizza type: Hawaiian (the most argued-about pizza, now unashamed)*

> **「FTX冇SFC牌照。**
> **HashKey有。**
> **我知我以前係咩立場。**
> **我錯咗。」**

**Behaviour profile:** Was previously the loudest voice against licensed exchanges at every crypto gathering. "Too slow. Too boring. Not how crypto was meant to work." Had exposure to FTX — not necessarily large, but enough. Spent time reconsidering. Now the loudest voice asking "do they have an SFC licence?" in every exchange recommendation thread. Does not apologise for the reversal. Does not need to.

**Crypto Twitter signature:** Posts honest "I was wrong" content that the community trusts precisely because it costs something to write. Refers to unlicensed exchanges in the past tense.

**Why people share this result:** The Reclaimed persona is the campaign's most strategically valuable. A converted skeptic sharing "I'm The Reclaimed" is the most credible compliance signal available — it cannot be manufactured by a brand. The persona is designed specifically to be claimed by people who have genuinely gone through this transition.

**Result card punchline:** *你終於係Hawaiian咗。歡迎。*
/ *You're Hawaiian now. Welcome.*

**Special role in campaign:** "你係Hawaiian未？" (Are you Hawaiian yet?) functions as the campaign's organic conversation starter. Any community member can ask it without brand involvement. The question implies a journey — and makes everyone who hasn't completed KYC aware that there is a journey to complete.

## 5.7.4 Physical Cards × SBT (Event Layer)

**Format:** Poker card size (63×88mm), double-sided. Front: persona character illustration + name. Back: QR code + SBT mint URL + one-line description.

**Distribution logic:** Scarcity is a design decision, not a production constraint. Personas are distributed at different volumes to create a natural hierarchy of cultural status.

| Card | Distribution | Unlock condition |
|---|---|---|
| 等跌黨 / DIP WAITER | Open distribution | Pick up at event entrance |
| 截圖選手 / P&L EXHIBITIONIST | Open distribution | Pick up at event entrance |
| 事後先知 / RETROACTIVE PROPHET | Open distribution | Pick up at event entrance |
| 撸毛黨 / AIRDROP FARMER | Limited | Complete HashKey KYC registration at event kiosk |
| 已覺醒者 / THE RECLAIMED | Rare | Answer "what exchange did you used to use and why did you switch?" at registration kiosk |

**The Reclaimed card unlock mechanics:** The question is not a barrier — it is a ritual. Saying out loud "I used to use [Binance / FTX / Bybit] and I switched because..." is the conversion narrative the campaign needs to be spoken, not just implied. The card is the reward for saying it.

### SBT Integration

Each physical card carries a QR code that leads to the SBT mint flow on HashKey's platform:

```
Scan card QR
→ Connect HashKey account (KYC required)
→ Mint Pizza Persona SBT to wallet
→ SBT visible on 0xU Founding Wall at event
→ On-chain proof of Pizza Day attendance
```

The Reclaimed SBT has a hard cap of 500 (event-night only issuance window). Scarcity is verifiable on-chain. "I have a Reclaimed SBT" becomes a community credential with a specific date stamp.

**LIHKG organic moment:** The card distribution system is designed to generate a specific kind of LIHKG post: "今日喺Central有張Hawaiian卡要你親口講自己以前用咩交易所先攞到，我話咗我以前用FTX，個工作人員笑咗，然後俾咗張卡我。" This kind of post — specific, embarrassing, humanising — cannot be manufactured. The mechanic is designed for it to happen organically.

## 5.7.6 Viral Propagation Strategy

The campaign does not seed personas through brand channels. It seeds **one question** and lets the community ask it of each other.

**The seed phrase:** 「你係Hawaiian未？」 *(Are you Hawaiian yet?)*

Deployment:
1. Laszlo Bot's Phase 1 Telegram message (February 2026): *「你踏馬來咗。你係Hawaiian未？」* — planted once, never repeated by the brand
2. The question travels organically in 0xU channel, then into broader HK crypto Telegram groups
3. LIHKG users encounter the Sham Shui Po Direction C poster: *「FTX冇SFC牌照。HashKey有。去搞清楚先。」* — creates the context for "你係Hawaiian未？" to mean something specific
4. Pizza Day event: Hawaiian card scarcity generates the LIHKG post. The post contains the phrase. The phrase spreads.

**Why this structure works:** The question is asked peer-to-peer, never brand-to-consumer. It carries no promotional language. It implies a journey everyone in the community recognises — and subtly suggests that if you haven't yet answered "yes," there is something you haven't sorted out.
