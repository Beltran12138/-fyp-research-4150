## COMM4150 FYP: Re-coding Trust | ZHAO Han (1155191400)

## Overview

A PR Audit is a systematic evaluation of an organisation's current communications activities, brand positioning, and media presence — establishing an evidence-based baseline from which strategic gaps can be identified. This audit examines HashKey Exchange across five dimensions: (1) corporate structure and brand architecture; (2) current messaging and tone of voice; (3) social media presence; (4) earned media and coverage profile; and (5) past PR campaigns. Findings are synthesised into a Gap Analysis that directly informs the "Re-coding Trust" campaign rationale in Section 4.

All quantitative data cited below reflects the period January 2024 – March 2026 unless otherwise noted.

---

## 3.1.1 Corporate Structure & Brand Architecture

HashKey Exchange operates as a subsidiary of **HashKey Group**, a vertically integrated digital asset conglomerate founded by Xiao Feng in 2018. The Group comprises five public-facing entities, each maintaining independent communications channels:

| Entity | Function | Primary Channel |
|---|---|---|
| HashKey Exchange (HK) | Licensed HK retail & professional trading | hashkey.com |
| HashKey Global | International retail trading (non-HK) | global.hashkey.com |
| HashKey Capital | VC / asset management | @HashKey_Capital (X) |
| HashKey OTC | Institutional over-the-counter desk | @HashKeyOTC (X) |
| HashKey Cloud | Node validation & staking | Separate subdomain |

**Regulatory milestones (PR anchors):** HashKey Exchange holds SFC Type 1 (dealing in securities) and Type 7 (automated trading services) licences, plus an AMLO licence obtained in May 2024, making it the first exchange in Hong Kong to hold all three — a "full-licence unicorn" in industry parlance. On December 17, 2025, HashKey Holdings listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange (HKEX: 3887), raising HK$1.6 billion (USD$206 million) at a USD$2.5 billion valuation — the first dedicated crypto exchange IPO in Asia. Combined, these credentials represent HashKey's most powerful owned PR assets.

**Brand architecture weakness:** The multi-entity structure produces fragmented communications. Operating @HashKeyExchange, @HashKey_Global, @HashKeyGroup, @HashKey_Capital, and @HashKeyOTC as independent X accounts — without a clear brand hierarchy, consistent visual identity across accounts, or unified narrative — creates audience confusion and dilutes share-of-voice. This contrasts with Binance's architecture of one flagship account with nested regional sub-accounts.

## 3.1.2 Brand Messaging & Tone of Voice

### Core Taglines

| Platform | Current Tagline / Headline |
|---|---|
| HashKey Exchange (HK) | **"Trading made Safe and Simple"** |
| HashKey Group (corporate) | **"Making Digital Assets Massively Accessible"** |
| HashKey Global (international) | "A Licensed and Regulated Crypto Exchange" |
| 2025 Institutional campaign | "Pioneering the Institutional Bridge Between TradFi and Crypto" |

### Messaging Pillars

HashKey's communications consistently orbit three pillars:

1. **Compliance-first safety**: SFC licensing, AMLO registration, institutional-grade security, and regulatory milestone announcements dominate both owned content and earned media coverage. Phrasing such as "on a mission to set the bar for virtual asset exchanges in compliance, safety, and security" (Company statement) frames these attributes as competitive advantages.

2. **Accessibility**: The Group-level mission and the "Safe and Simple" tagline both signal a desire to lower barriers to entry. However, the *practised* accessibility is limited — as of early 2025, Hong Kong retail users could only access approximately 20–25 trading pairs, compared to Binance's 350+ — creating a gap between the stated aspiration and the lived product experience.

3. **Institutional bridge**: The 2025 Digital Asset Treasury (DAT) strategy introduced a fourth messaging frame targeting TradFi capital: "Pioneering the Institutional Bridge Between TradFi and Crypto" (PRNewswire, September 2025). This represents a deliberate pivot toward institutional PR, reinforcing existing strengths but further distancing the brand from the retail degen community.

### Tone of Voice Analysis

HashKey's communications are characterised by a **formal, announcement-driven, compliance-forward** register. Key indicators:

- Press releases are distributed through established wire services (PRNewswire, Globe Newswire) — appropriate for institutional audiences, invisible to crypto-native communities who distrust "corporate press."
- Social posts are predominantly product announcements, partnership MOUs, and regulatory milestone declarations.
- CEO/executive communications from Xiao Feng skew toward policy commentary and institutional investor dialogue.
- There is negligible use of meme formats, slang, or community-native language on any owned channel.

This tone profile is coherent with HashKey's institutional positioning but functionally excludes the 18–35 retail segment. As documented in Section 2.1.1 (Computational Social Listening), HashKey's semantic cluster is dominated by "SFC," "licence," "compliance," and "insurance" — while the target demographic's discourse centres on "airdrop," "degen," "meme coins," and "gas fee." The linguistic worlds do not intersect.

## 3.1.3 Social Media Presence

### Platform-by-Platform Audit

**Twitter / X**

| @HashKey_Global | ~245,900 | Institutional | Listings, partnerships, regulatory updates |
| @HashKeyExchange | Not publicly confirmed | Institutional | HK-specific compliance announcements |
| @HashKey_Capital | Not publicly confirmed | Institutional | VC investment thesis, Web3 thought leadership |
| @HashKeyOTC | Not publicly confirmed | Institutional | OTC trading desk updates |

The @HashKey_Global figure of approximately 245,900 followers represents roughly **2.3% of Binance's reach** (~10.6 million) and **27% of Bybit's** (~907,000). Accounting for the relative difference in user base size, the per-user engagement rate on social channels is a critical indicator: Binance and Bybit sustain social communities proportionally far larger than their user base through meme campaigns, sports sponsorships (Bybit: Formula 1), and crypto-cultural content. HashKey does not.

**Telegram**

HashKey Global maintains an official Telegram channel (t.me/HashKey_Global). In 2024, HashKey launched a **tap-to-earn Telegram bot** offering up to 300 $HSK tokens to new users through a mini-game mechanic — aligning with Web3-native user acquisition tactics popularised by The Open Network (TON) ecosystem (The Block, 2024). The initiative demonstrates awareness of Telegram as a community platform, but specific channel subscriber figures are not publicly available, suggesting the presence is modest relative to the ambition. No large-scale open community discussion group on the scale of Binance's regional Telegram groups has been identified.

**LinkedIn**

| Entity | Followers |
|---|---|
| HashKey Group | ~16,013 |
| HashKey Exchange | ~700 |

Content is executive thought leadership, regulatory milestone announcements, and institutional partnership news — appropriate for B2B audiences but not a retail acquisition channel.

**YouTube**

No substantive YouTube strategy was identified. HashKey does not maintain a comparable equivalent to Binance Academy, Bybit Learn, or Coinbase's educational content programmes. The absence of an owned long-form video channel represents a missed opportunity for organic search traffic, brand authority, and retail audience acquisition through financial education content.

**WeChat & Xiaohongshu (Little Red Book)**

No confirmed official WeChat Official Account or Xiaohongshu presence was identified. Given that Chinese-speaking Hongkongers and mainland Chinese diaspora form a natural and primary target demographic, and that WeChat has near-100% penetration in this demographic, this absence is a structural communications gap. Competitors including OKX, Gate.io, and HTX maintain active Chinese-language social ecosystems on both platforms.

## 3.1.4 Earned Media Coverage

### Coverage Volume and Outlets

HashKey receives consistent earned media coverage across both institutional financial media and crypto-specialist outlets. The primary coverage ecosystem is:

**Mainstream financial press:** South China Morning Post (most consistent), Bloomberg, Fortune, CNBC — primarily triggered by milestone events (IPO, funding rounds, regulatory approvals).

**Crypto-specialist press:** CoinDesk, The Block, BeInCrypto, Blockhead — regulatory analysis, token economics, competitive positioning.

**Chinese-language crypto press:** PANews, Followin, AiCoin — the most detailed ongoing coverage of internal developments, particularly financial performance and HR matters.

**HK fintech press:** FintechNews HK, TechNode Global, Caproasia — ecosystem and partnership coverage.

### Dominant Narrative Frames

| Frame | Outlets | Valence |
|---|---|---|
| "The Compliant Exchange" | All outlets | Positive / Neutral |
| "Hong Kong's Crypto Flagship" | SCMP, BeInCrypto | Positive |
| "Asia's First Crypto IPO" | Bloomberg, Fortune, CNBC | Positive (historically) |
| "Loss-Making Market Leader" | CoinDesk, PANews, AiCoin | Negative |
| "Beijing's Watchful Eye" | SCMP (Dec 2025) | Mixed / Cautious |

**The compliance frame** is the strongest and most durable narrative: HashKey is consistently positioned as the institutional exemplar of Hong Kong's crypto regulatory experiment. This is genuine competitive differentiation in institutional media — but as established in Section 2.2.1, "compliance" ranked last among Hong Kong retail investors' exchange selection factors (M=3.12/5.0), meaning the earned media frames that reinforce compliance messaging are simultaneously impotent for the target demographic.

**The loss-making frame** is the most persistent reputational challenge: CoinDesk's December 2025 headline — "HashKey Leads Hong Kong's Crypto Market as Losses Deepen Ahead of IPO" — directly juxtaposed market leadership (75% HK market share, HK$638.4 billion trading volume in 2024) with financial fragility (USD$151 million net loss in 2024). Monthly compliance costs reported at approximately USD$10 million render the low-fee competitive strategy structurally challenging. While HashKey's standard response frames losses as necessary investment in compliance infrastructure and market-building, this counter-narrative lacks emotional resonance in a market that responds to growth stories.

### Crisis Case: The $HSK Token Collapse (2025)

The most significant reputational crisis during the audit period was the collapse of HashKey's native platform token $HSK, which declined approximately **85%** from USD$2.38 (December 2024) to USD$0.35 (May 2025), with further erosion to USD$0.25 by year-end 2025 (CoinMarketCap). The crisis presented three intersecting dimensions:

1. **Employee welfare**: Over 200 current and former employees reportedly held $HSK as a component of their salary compensation. The token's collapse effectively constituted a significant pay cut for these individuals, creating an internal morale crisis with external reputational implications.

2. **Operational allegations**: Reports from Chinese-language crypto media alleged layoffs affecting one-third of staff and delayed payments to IT suppliers. HashKey issued denials asserting its global workforce remained above 600 employees. The denial-without-positive-narrative pattern is a characteristically reactive crisis communications posture — technically accurate but PR-ineffective.

3. **Cash flow reporting**: An anonymous institutional source cited reported cash reserves equivalent to "approximately six months of cash flow remaining" (AiCoin, 2025). HashKey did not formally confirm or refute this characterisation.

**Crisis communications assessment:** The $HSK episode reveals a structural PR vulnerability created by the conflation of the compliance-forward exchange brand with a speculative token economy. The core communications failure was that the token had been integrated into employee compensation without a corresponding communications strategy to manage the reputational exposure created by that decision. The crisis was addressable at the structural level (separating exchange brand from token brand in communications) but was instead handled reactively at the incident level.

## 3.1.5 Past PR Campaigns & Activations

### Bitcoin Pizza Day 2025 — Free Pizza Truck (May 22, 2025)
**Format:** Guerrilla / experiential street activation
**Execution:** At the moment Bitcoin surpassed USD$110,000, HashKey deployed a free pizza truck touring key Hong Kong commercial districts (Central Pier 9, Haiphong Road, Tsim Sha Tsui). The activation tied the contemporary Bitcoin price milestone explicitly to the 2010 Laszlo Hanyecz transaction (10,000 BTC for two pizzas), creating a narrative that spanned Bitcoin's history.
**Coverage:** AiCoin, Chinese-language crypto media.
**Assessment:** The most creatively significant consumer-facing PR activation in HashKey's recent history. It demonstrates an understanding of Bitcoin Pizza Day as a cultural moment with community resonance — and establishes proof of concept that HashKey is willing and operationally capable of executing street-level brand activations. However, the activation was a one-day tactical event without an integrated campaign structure, documented outcome metrics, or sustained social media follow-through. The cultural insight was right; the strategic deployment was underdeveloped.

### Hong Kong Web3 Festival 2024 & 2025 — Co-Host
**Format:** Major industry conference
**Scale:** 160+ projects, 100+ media outlets, 300+ speakers (2024); revenue HK$37.1 million.
**Assessment:** Co-hosting (not merely sponsoring) the Web3 Festival positions HashKey as a civic institution in Hong Kong's Web3 ecosystem rather than simply a commercial exchange — a deliberate "pillar of the community" PR posture that generates B2B and policy-level credibility. However, Web3 Festival audiences skew toward institutional, developer, and investor profiles. Consumer / retail youth community activation is not a strength of this channel. The festival PR posture reinforces the institutional frame; it does not address the cultural gap.

### TON/Telegram Partnership (2024)
**Format:** Strategic partnership announcement
**Content:** HashKey signed an agreement with The Open Network (TON) blockchain to serve as the licensed fiat on/off-ramp for Toncoin transactions in Asia (SCMP, 2024).
**Assessment:** A strategically significant announcement with natural PR synergy to Telegram's approximately 950 million global users. The community-native Telegram/TON ecosystem is precisely the "degen-adjacent" demographic that HashKey struggles to reach through its standard institutional messaging. The partnership was covered but not amplified with community-targeted content. Structural PR opportunity underutilised.

### Digital Asset Treasury (DAT) Strategy Launch (September 2025)
**Format:** Wire distribution PR campaign
**Content:** USD$500 million first-phase fund announcement targeting institutional TradFi capital. Distributed via PRNewswire to Morningstar, CoinDesk, Manila Times, and regional financial outlets.
**Assessment:** Appropriate institutional PR execution. Not relevant to the 18–35 retail audience.

### $HSK Token Distribution via Telegram Bot (2024)
**Format:** Community/gamified acquisition
**Content:** Tap-to-earn Telegram mini-game distributing up to 300 $HSK tokens to new users.
**Assessment:** The only documented attempt at crypto-native community acquisition mechanics during the audit period. The tap-to-earn format is native to the degen audience and demonstrated tactical awareness of Telegram as a community channel. The subsequent collapse of $HSK value retroactively undermined this initiative's ability to create positive community associations.

## 3.1.6 Gap Analysis

Synthesising the above audit against the Section 2 research findings, five structural PR gaps are identified:

### Gap 1: The Cultural Register Gap
**Evidence:** HashKey's tone of voice is institutional and compliance-centric across all channels. Survey data (N=287, Section 2.2.1) documents "Lack of Degen Culture" as the #1 barrier to licensed exchange adoption (33.8%), while word association analysis confirms "Boring" (39.4%) as the dominant cultural perception of the brand. LBank Benchmark data (Section 2.3) establishes that culture — not compliance — drives both acquisition and long-term retention in this demographic.
**Gap:** HashKey communicates exclusively in the language of regulation. The target demographic (18–35) resides in a semantic ecosystem of memes, airdrops, and cultural narrative. No current communications vehicle bridges this gap.

### Gap 2: The Social Media Scale & Engagement Gap
**Evidence:** @HashKey_Global holds ~245,900 X followers vs. Binance's ~10.6 million and Bybit's ~907,000. No YouTube strategy. No WeChat / Xiaohongshu presence. No open community Telegram group of significant scale.
**Gap:** Social media architecture is built for announcement broadcast, not community cultivation. The infrastructure for organic community engagement — essential for the 18–35 demographic — does not currently exist.

### Gap 3: The Chinese-Language Platform Gap
**Evidence:** No confirmed WeChat Official Account or Xiaohongshu presence despite Chinese-speaking Hongkongers and mainland Chinese diaspora being a primary target audience.
**Gap:** HashKey is effectively absent from the platforms most used by its core demographic for financial information and community formation.

### Gap 4: The Youth-Targeted Content Gap
**Evidence:** No YouTube educational channel, no content series targeting younger investors, no editorial programme building brand authority among first-time crypto investors.
**Gap:** Competitors are building retail brand authority and organic traffic through long-form educational content (Binance Academy, Bybit Learn). HashKey does not participate in this content category.

### Gap 5: The Pizza Day Opportunity Gap
**Evidence:** Section 2.1.1 temporal sentiment analysis identified Bitcoin Pizza Day (May 22) as the single highest engagement anomaly in the HK Web3 community calendar (Mean Polarity shifts from neutral/cautious baseline to a measurable engagement peak). The 2025 pizza truck activation demonstrated operational execution capability and institutional willingness to engage. Survey data (Section 2.2.1) confirms 63.1% combined awareness of Pizza Day among the target demographic (72.5% among 18–24 cohort).
**Gap:** HashKey has demonstrated proof-of-concept for Pizza Day activation (2025 truck) but has not built an integrated campaign structure that converts the cultural moment into durable brand repositioning. The cultural insight exists; the strategic framework does not.
