## 8.1 Budget Framework

The campaign budget is structured to reflect the channel architecture's fundamental logic: the largest single expenditure category is the airdrop, because the airdrop is simultaneously the campaign's primary conversion mechanic and its most cost-efficient acquisition tool. The second largest category is OOH paid media — the campaign's only above-the-line investment, concentrated in financial district and youth culture locations for Segment B (Cautious Explorers). Production, technology, and KOL investments are calibrated to the minimum required to execute each pillar at credibility threshold — not maximised, because over-production of materials designed to appear organic undermines the Safe Punk aesthetic that gives them value.

All costs are expressed in Hong Kong Dollars (HKD). Where unit costs are variable (e.g., OOH rate-card ranges), the midpoint of the confirmed booking range is used. The airdrop allocation is treated as a marketing expenditure rather than a product cost: the 10,001 HKX token distribution is the campaign's primary conversion incentive, directly generating KYC-completed account registrations (Objective C1). Belch and Belch (2021) define promotional allowances and incentive programmes as legitimate IMC expenditure items when they produce measurable audience action — the airdrop meets this definition (Chapter 14).

## 8.4 Budget Allocation Rationale

**OOH-heavy above-the-line investment (29.8%)** reflects the No Paid Social Principle: the campaign's sole conventional paid media investment is concentrated in physical channels where promotional content does not trigger the cultural antibody response that paid social produces in crypto-native audiences (Section 4.5.5; Section 6, Finding 4). MTR Central and Causeway Bay placements serve Segment B (Cautious Explorers) — financial district workers and youth culture consumers whose discovery pathway runs through physical space, not algorithmic feeds.

**Airdrop as acquisition investment (22.4%)** reflects the dual function of the 10,001 mechanic: it is simultaneously a community formation signal (scarcity + belonging), a conversion incentive (KYC completion required), and a token distribution event (decentralised community building). Allen, Berg, and Lane (2023) identify airdrop campaigns as generating measurably lower cost-per-engaged-user than conventional digital advertising in Web3 contexts — the 10,001 cap amplifies this by creating demand pressure that converts passive interest into active registration.

**Technology investment (12.7%)** reflects the campaign's architecture: four of the seven execution pillars are technology-dependent (Laszlo Bot, RIB Microsite, Pizza Personas, Developer Infrastructure). Unlike production costs, these are permanent assets: the microsite, bot, and developer infrastructure continue to generate value post-campaign.

**The absence of paid social spend** is a deliberate allocation choice, not a budget residual. The ~HK$400,000–600,000 that would typically be allocated to promoted posts and sponsored content has been reallocated to the airdrop and technology, which produce measurable community formation outcomes that paid impressions cannot.

## 8.5 Cost-Per-Acquisition Analysis

| Objective | Target | Budget Allocated | Effective CPA (HKD) |
|---|---|---|---|
| C1 — KYC airdrop accounts (10,001) | 10,001 accounts | Airdrop: 530,050 + Event: 260,000 | HK$79 per KYC account |
| C2 — Net-new registrations (3,000) | 3,000 accounts | Attributable spend ≈ 790,050 | HK$263 per attributed registration |
| E1 — TG community (5,000 members) | 5,000 members | TG channel + Bot: 85,000 + KOL: 215,000 | HK$60 per member |

The C1 effective CPA of HK$79 per KYC-completed account reflects the campaign's efficiency advantage over conventional paid acquisition: the airdrop scarcity mechanic produces voluntary, KYC-motivated account registrations without requiring paid media to drive each conversion. The C2 CPA of HK$263, while higher, is below the estimated HK$200–400 industry benchmark for equivalent licensed exchange registrations through paid digital channels — and the campaign-acquired users carry significantly higher community attachment and retention probability (Objective C3: ≥40% 30-day active trading rate).
